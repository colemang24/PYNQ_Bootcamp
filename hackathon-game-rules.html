Games

We will be playing three different games as fun activities to break up the hackathon:

  1. MS Paint
  2. Airplanes
  3. Memory Game

MS Paint

Prove your artistic skill.

Prerequisites

  • Laptop with MS Paint for each student
  • Bob Ross YouTube video
  • Room with projector
  • Voting Tokens (three post-it notes per student)

Setup

  1. Every student opens MS Paint on their laptop.
  2. Organizers play back Bob Ross video on the projector. Make sure all students are ready before starting to play.
  3. Periodically remind students to save their work.
  4. As students are painting, organizers distribute three voting tokens to each student.
  5. After video is done, students get five extra minutes for finishing touches to their masterpieces.
  6. Students roam around looking at other's artwork. They vote for their favorites by placing their tokens next to the laptops showing the artwork they like.
  7. The student who receives most tokens wins. Display their artwork on the projector.

Rules

  1. No painting after the five minute cut-off!
  2. You may only use MS Paint, no other painting software and no other aids.
  3. You may not vote for your own artwork.
  4. You may give multiple of your tokens to the same artwork if you especially like it.

Paper Airplanes

Who can build the most aerodynamic plane?

Prerequisites

  • Paper
  • Table/workspace on which students can fold planes
  • Good outside location to launch the planes

Setup

  1. Distribute three sheets of paper to every student.
  2. Give students 15 minutes to fold three paper airplanes.
  3. Every student puts an identifying mark on the wings of their plane (e.g. their name).
  4. Head to launch site.
  5. Students go one by one launching their first plane, from the same location, in the same target direction.
  6. After all stundents launched their first plane, judges evaluate which plane flew farthest in the target direction and take note.
  7. Repeat the same for the next two paper planes of each student.
  8. Judges announce the constructor of the plane that flew the farthest overall as the winner of the competition.

Rules

  1. All students launch their planes from the same location in the same direction.
  2. You are judged based on distance travelled in the target direction. If a plane flies far, but it goes the wrong direction, that does not count.
  3. The best run of the three planes is the one that counts.

Memory Game

Show off your memorization skills!

Prerequisites

  • Table
  • a cloth
  • 15-20 random items
  • timer
  • Pen & paper

Setup / Rules

  1. Give a paper and pen to each student.
  2. Take a set of 15-20 items and place them on a table.
  3. Allow students to study all the items for 25-30 seconds.
  4. Then place a cloth on all the items.
  5. Start the timer for 50/60 seconds.
  6. Students will write the list of articles they memorize within the limit.
  7. Winner is the one that gets the highest count.
